Chicago: The Black Experience

Chicago is a melting pot for multiple cultures, but the culture of Black people in Chicago is one that is important and extremely personal. There are so many organizations, restaurants, and events ran by Black people that have a positive impact on the community.


Bud Billiken Parade

The Bud Billiken® Parade is the largest African American parade in the United States. It takes place right on the South Side of Chicago going down King Drive. The parade has taken place every year since 1929 and it has united African-Americans throughout the city of Chicago in music, dance, food, and laughter since the start. This parade allows youth to show the talents they have been practicing over the summer leading up to the parade and it helps youth celebrate their return to school. Each year the parade has over 100 organizations participate in the parade. Students also receive school supplies to help them with resources for the start of school. The Bud Billiken is near and dear to my heart because as a kid, I went to the parade every year with my aunt and cousins. The Bud Billiken is notorious for bringing the Black community together.



Chicago Jazz Festival

The Chicago Jazz Festival is one of the oldest of Chicago’s free lakefront music festivals. This free festival gives people the opportunity to come out and enjoy soulful music while being immersed in the community. The lineup for this festival has consisted of jazz legends and soon-to-be legends. The show takes place in Millenium park and usually last 2-3 days.



The Taste of Chicago

The Taste of Chicago is the world’s largest free-admission outdoor food festival. The festival was started over 40 years ago and it has continuously grown over the years. This festival gives citizens and tourists the opportunity to get a taste of Chicago specialties all in one place. Multiple vendors participate in this festival through pop-up shops and food trucks. Food is not the only thing you get at the Taste of Chicago! There is also entertainment such as dance performances, art showcases, and live singing performances. The only catch is that attendees do have to purchase tickets in order to participate and indulge in the delicious food. The experience is well worth it!
