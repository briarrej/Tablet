Chicago: The Black Experience

Chicago is a melting pot for multiple cultures, but the culture of Black people in Chicago is one that is important and extremely personal. There are so many organizations, restaurants, and events ran by Black people that have a positive impact on the community.


My Block, My Hood, My City

My Block, My Hood, My City’s (M3) purpose is “to inspire youth, empower communities, and build a better world one block at a time.” This organization motivates everyone to take action and do something to better their community. M3 emphasizes the importance of exploring new communities and cultures to truly gain a better understanding of the world. Within M3, there is a program, Explorers Program, that gives youth from deprived communities opportunities to explore Vocation/Education, Arts Expression, Culinary Experiences, and Recreation sites within the city of Chicago and beyond. This exposure helps open the youth’s minds to things outside of their community.


EmpowerYouth!

EmpowerYouth! is a program through the Chicago Urban League where 30 African American high school students work to put on a performance. This program is in partnership with the Lyrica Opera of Chicago and it allows students exposure to different art forms such as opera, acting, singing, dancing, and more. Students are able to express themselves through the arts and learn new art forms, which are showcased in the final performance. EmpowerYouth! gives Black students a space to be creative and have an amazing support system filled with resources.


Social Works

Social Works is a non-profit organization formed by Chance the Rapper, an African American artist who is Chicago Native. Since 2016, Social Works has aimed to empower the youth through the arts, education, and civic engagement. Social Works has 5 initiatives: Support CPS, OpenMike, My State of Mind, Warmest Winter, and Kids of the Kingdom. One of my favorite initiatives is the OpenMike, where high school students can showcase their talents through a 3-minute piece of their choice. This opportunity gives students a chance to gain exposure and perform in front of like-minded peers.
